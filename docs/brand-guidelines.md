# Brand Guidelines

## CareerEdge's peak moments — and what delight looks like at each:
1. The roadmap reveal (onboarding, Screen B)
This is the highest-stakes peak in the entire product. First real impression. The delight here is the reveal itself — not confetti, not animation for animation's sake. The roadmap materialises with presence. Sophia's cyan glow arrives before she speaks. The user feels like something was made for them. Restraint here is the delight — not emptiness, not spectacle.
2. The dashboard unlock (H3 specifically — blur lifts)
Lime pulse. One moment. 600ms. EdgeGas +10 toast slides in. Gone in under a second. The product acknowledges the user crossed a threshold. That's it.
3. Milestone completions on EdgePath
A phase completing is a genuine achievement. This earns a celebration — but scaled to the moment. Completing Phase 1 of a 6-month roadmap is not a confetti cannon. It's a brief lime pulse on the phase card, Sophia's bar text updating to acknowledge it, and an EdgeGas credit that feels earned. The next phase unlocking with a subtle entrance animation is the real reward — forward momentum is the delight.
4. The EdgeGas earn moment (any trigger)
This is the only place lime is expressive. Brief radial pulse from the sidebar EdgeGas indicator. The number ticks up. A toast: "⚡ +5 — resume completed." Spring animation, 400ms. Gone. The restraint of every other moment makes this one legible.
5. The first job match (EdgeMatch, first visit with a complete profile)
Sophia's bar text shifts: "23 roles match your profile right now." Not a notification badge — a sentence. The match percentage chip on the first card is amber (#F59E0B, EdgeStar accent). That number, specific to them, is the delight. Data as delight.

## What the user came here to accomplish
Three lenses. The design must serve all three, but the visual hierarchy picks one to lead.

Functional Job
Understand what my next move is
The user is confused about their career path, stuck between options, or just left a job. They need a concrete, credible picture of what to do next — not advice, a plan.
Emotional Job
Feel like someone smart is guiding me
They don't want another job board. They want to feel like they found something that actually understands their situation. The product needs to feel intelligent, not generic.
Social Job
Have something worth sharing or saving
A roadmap with their name on it, their career, their level. Something that feels like it was built for them — not a template. This drives both D7 return and referral sharing.
