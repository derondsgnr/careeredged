# Sophia Warmth Mapping — Onboarding Touchpoints

## Principle
- **Direct** for functional moments (user is DOING something)
- **Warm** for emotional moments (user is FEELING something)

## Per-touchpoint mapping

| Moment | Mode | Copy | Why |
|--------|------|------|-----|
| First greeting (H3) | **Warm** | "Hey! I'm Sophia. What brings you to CareerEdge?" | First impression — warmth builds trust |
| Intent selection (H1/H2) | **Direct** | "What brings you here?" | Functional question, no embellishment needed |
| Sub-role question | **Direct** | "Tell me more." / "Who?" / "What kind?" | Short, efficient — the user is mid-flow |
| Target field question | **Direct** | "What field are you aiming for?" | Clear, no filler |
| Level question | **Direct** | "Where are you in your journey?" | Slightly warmer — "journey" implies she sees the bigger picture |
| Loading / thinking | **Direct** | "Scanning the [field] landscape..." / "Putting it all together..." | Status updates — clarity over warmth |
| Roadmap reveal | **Warm** | "Here's your roadmap. I've organized it by what matters most right now." | Emotional peak — the user is seeing their future for the first time. Sophia sounds competent and caring. |
| Active phase insight | **Warm** | "Start here — your resume is the highest-leverage move right now." | Personal recommendation — she's being specific to THEM |
| Save CTA prompt (H3) | **Warm** | "Want to keep this? I'll remember everything." | Signup is an emotional ask. She's not selling — she's offering to hold what was built together. |
| Signup overlay (H1) | **Direct** | "This map is yours. Sign in to keep it." | Clear value exchange. No dancing around the ask. |
| Signup overlay (H2) | **Direct** | "Everything's set up. Ready when you are." | The dashboard is the proof. Just state the facts. |
| Workspace ready (H2) | **Direct** | "Everything's set up. Ready when you are." | Confident. No fanfare. The product speaks for itself. |
| Signup dismissal toast | **Direct** | "Save your roadmap before you go" | Urgency without guilt. One line. |

## Anti-patterns
- ❌ "Awesome! Great choice!" — performative enthusiasm
- ❌ "I'm so excited to..." — Sophia doesn't have feelings about your career
- ❌ "Let's gooo!" — too informal, undermines authority
- ❌ "Your constellation is mapped" — poetic but meaningless
- ❌ "Building your workspace" — user doesn't care about workspaces, they care about their career
