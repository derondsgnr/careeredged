## The CareerEdge Visual DNA (from Figma brand guidelines)

Before any references — this is what we have to work with:

### Hero Direction (Figma slide 45 — laptop mockup)
- **Dark canvas** (Space #00253A / near-black)
- **White headline** with Green Yellow (#B3FF3B) accent on the emotionally-loaded word ("belong")
- **Subheadline** in muted gray
- **Green Yellow CTA button** ("Get Your Edge →") + ghost secondary ("See how it works")
- **Interactive search bar** below hero with category tabs (Transition, Skill Gap, Career, Network, Coach)
- **Nav:** Logo left, center links (cyan active states), Green Yellow CTA right

### Brand Asset Patterns (how they're already used)
1. **Photography clipped into geometric shapes** — The logo's arrow shape becomes a photo mask. Hexagonal/arrow cutouts frame real people. Creates editorial feel.
2. **Brand Pattern 01** — Arrow element repeated into DNA strand. Used on Fresh Sky (#14A9FF) backgrounds.
3. **Brand Pattern 02** — Geometric grid derived from logo. Used on Green Yellow (#B3FF3B) surfaces.
4. **Dark Space panels on bright surfaces** — Tote bag, social cards use Space (#00253A) text blocks sitting on Green Yellow pattern backgrounds.
5. **Photography on blue fields** — Social cards/comms show photos on Fresh Sky backgrounds with data stats overlaid (10%, 25%).
6. **Billboard messaging** — Large type + photography in geometric frames + brand pattern texture. "Go beyond job searching. **Build your career.**"

### Photography Rules
- Clean, well-lit, natural depth of field
- Diverse professionals — real-world success, not staged
- Never: distorted, tinted with off-brand colors, overly filtered, desaturated, or low-res
- Editorial feel — "communicates both the human impact and the technical precision"

## Reference Sites — Ranked by Match

### Tier 1: Strongest matches (study these deeply)

#### 1. Masterclass (masterclass.com)
**Why it matches:**
- **Dark hero** with real photography of real people — not product screenshots
- **Split layout:** bold headline left, large portrait right
- **Interactive element below hero** — "What brings you to MasterClass today?" with selectable options (maps to CareerEdge's search bar + category tabs)
- **Red accent CTA** on dark background (analogous to Green Yellow on Space)
- **Premium editorial photography** throughout
- **Multi-audience** — different people, different goals, one platform

**What to steal:**
- The hero composition: bold type + real person + interactive qualifier
- How photography LEADS the visual hierarchy, not product screenshots
- The "choose your path" interaction pattern below the fold
- The premium-but-accessible tone

---

#### 2. Shopify (shopify.com)
**Why it matches:**
- **Green accent** (Shopify green) as the signature brand color — used on CTAs and highlights
- **Photography of real people** — actual merchants, named founders, real businesses
- **Multi-stakeholder platform** — merchants, partners, developers, enterprise. Different hero messaging for different audiences.
- **Rotating hero tagline** — "Be the next big [store they line up for / unicorn startup / household name]" — dynamic, aspirational
- **Clean white space** with bold typography

**What to steal:**
- How they serve multiple audiences without diluting the hero
- Named real people with real titles = instant credibility
- The green CTA conviction — it's ALWAYS green, it's ALWAYS visible
- How the Solutions dropdown cleanly segments stakeholders

---

#### 3. Deel (deel.com)
**Why it matches:**
- **Dark hero** (deep purple/black) with accent-colored keyword ("anywhere" in purple)
- **Split hero layout** — headline + CTA left, social proof/testimonial right
- **Multi-stakeholder** — "Solutions" and "Who we serve" dropdowns in nav
- **Pill badge** above headline ("Skip the sales call. Hire your first employee now.")
- **Enterprise positioning** with warm brand feel

**What to steal:**
- The dark hero with accent-colored word — directly maps to CareerEdge's "belong" in Green Yellow
- Pill badge / eyebrow text pattern
- How testimonials sit RIGHT in the hero (not buried below)
- The nav structure: Solutions dropdown + audience segmentation

#### 6. Pitch (pitch.com)

- Dual CTA pattern (primary colored + secondary ghost)

## Design Principles for All 3 Variations

1. **Photography leads** — Every major section includes real people. Not as decoration. As the emotional core.
2. **Dark hero, always** — The Figma exploration established this. All 3 variations start on Space/near-black.
3. **Green Yellow is sacred** — Only on CTAs, accent words, and earned moments. Never decorative.
4. **Brand shapes frame imagery** — The logo-derived hexagonal/arrow shapes clip photography. This is the visual signature.
5. **Brand patterns as texture** — Arrow DNA pattern and geometric grid as subtle backgrounds, never overwhelming.
6. **Alternating rhythm** — Dark → Light → Dark → Light sections create visual breathing room.
7. **Scanner-first** — Bold headlines visible at scroll speed. Details only for those who stop.
